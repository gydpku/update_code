
In providing oral health care for infants, children, adolescents, the ability of using both nonpharmacological and pharmacological behavior guidance techniques is fundamental for pediatric dentists. The various behavior guidance techniques used must be tailored to the individual patient and practitioner. Promoting a positive dental attitude, safety, and quality of care are of the utmost importance. In this chapter, many behavior guidance techniques used in contemporary pediatric dentistry are expounded, including sedation and general anesthesia.
