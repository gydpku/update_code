
The most commonly seen temporomandibular joint diseases are temporomandibular disorders (TMD) and inflammatory diseases. Their etiology is complex. Clinically, pain in the TMJ area, abnormality in mandibular movement and clicking are usually present. The pathological changes of osteoarthritis include degenerations of the meniscus, condylar cartilage and bony tissues. Rheumatoid osteoarthritis is one of the local manifestations of the systematic joint disease. The synovial chondromatosis is a tumor-like lesion, true tumor lesions are rare in TMJ.
