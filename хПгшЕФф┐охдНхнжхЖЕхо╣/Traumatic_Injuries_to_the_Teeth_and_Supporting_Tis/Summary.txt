
Dental trauma, characterized by its diversity and complexity, may involve dental hard tissue, pulp-periodontal tissue and even peripheral alveolar and jaw bone. Further, being in the dynamic stages of growth and development, children of different stages might suffer varied effects from similar dental trauma. When dealing with children with dental trauma, attention should be paid to damages to dental hard tissue as well as to pulp-periodontal tissue and peripheral supporting tissues Root development condition, dentition and occlusal features of children should also be taken into consideration in drawing a comprehensive assessment of long-term influence on the physical and psychological health of children.
